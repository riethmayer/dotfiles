# Part 3: Create Your Strategic Roadmap

Based on the RDCL Toolkit v3.1.0 (CC BY-SA 4.0) by radicalproduct.com.

## Instructions

Group together items from your chosen RDCL Strategy Canvas into activity-focused **Initiatives** and assign them to responsible individuals and teams.

### Example Initiatives

| Initiative | Team |
|---|---|
| Validate Key Pain Points | UX Research Team |
| Establish Customer Relationships | Head of UX + Head of Sales |
| Develop Critical Technology | Engineering |
| Improve UX of Key Flows | Design + Engineering |
| Rebrand and Redesign Website | Marketing + Copywriter |

### Milestones: Now / Next / Later

For each Initiative, identify key milestones across three time horizons:

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Now** | Immediate priorities. For a startup: weeks. For enterprise: next quarter(s). |
| **Next** | Medium-term. The step after "Now" is achieved. |
| **Later** | Long-term. Aspirational but directionally correct. |

### Writing good milestones
- Use **past tense** to describe a concrete moment in time: "Initial product launched to critical mass of pre-sales customers"
- A milestone is a **concrete step** toward achieving the Initiative as a whole
- Ensure timelines make sense **across all Initiatives** — this is your opportunity to align and coordinate different teams

### When to revisit
- Every 2-3 months, or more often if there are major uncertainties
- Your **vision should never fundamentally change**
- Your **strategy should evolve only with great deliberation**
- Your **roadmap needs to respond** to changing conditions regularly

## Output format

```markdown
## Strategic Roadmap

Date: {today}

| Initiative | Responsible | Now ({timeline}) | Next ({timeline}) | Later ({timeline}) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| {name} | {team/person} | {milestone} | {milestone} | {milestone} |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```
