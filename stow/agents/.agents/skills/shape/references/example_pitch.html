Prompt Library

An institutional memory you can install. Every department contributes the skills they use to do their job; engineering blesses what's safe; everyone runs them in the tool they already use.

A skill library is org design. Standards, conventions, and the workflows that move the needle get codified once and reused everywhere. New joiners onboard against the workflows their team actually uses.
Why now

Every Earlybirder already has prompts and skills on their laptop that materially improve their work. There's no way to share the good ones, vet them for safety, or get updates as they improve. We don't just have a sharing problem — we have a missing institutional layer. Skills are the cheapest unit of org knowledge: small enough to write in an afternoon, specific enough to encode our jargon, durable enough to outlive the person who wrote them.

Generic catalogs like skills.sh can't fill this gap. The skills that matter reference our data tables, our pipeline stages, our deal flow. The library has to be ours. The blessing workflow (engineering reviews for security + quality + PII) keeps it safe; the org-specific content makes it valuable.

The pitch

For Earlybirders — install a CLI, run ebsk sync, the skills your workspace has blessed appear in your tools. Author one yourself in 20 minutes; ebsk submit sends it for review; once blessed, it ships to everyone on the team automatically.

For other VC firms and companies — same product, self-hosted. Apache 2.0. Brand attribution in the README. Earlybird becomes the reference implementation other firms point at when they stand up their own.

For the partnership — the artifact we leave behind every quarter is a measurable lift in "how Earlybird actually works." We can name the workflows that are codified. We can show the velocity gain in onboarding. We have the public surface (skills.earlybird.com) that demonstrates Earlybird-as-AI-native to the LP and founder audiences that care.

"I wrote a five-line skill to grill myself on a new investment thesis. Three weeks later half the partnership runs it before every Monday meeting. That doesn't happen with Google Docs." — anticipated, partner, Q3 2026
How it works
Author
Run ebsk init grill-thesis in your terminal. Edit a single SKILL.md on your laptop. Iterate freely — nothing leaves your machine unless you opt in to private cloud backup.
Submit
ebsk submit grill-thesis --workspace=investment opens a PR against the private skills repo. Engineering admins run the blessing checklist: hooks · PII · evals · visibility.
Bless
Merge = deploy. The next ebsk sync on every workspace member's laptop installs it. Unbless = uninstall on next sync. The blessing IS the deployment.
Use
In Claude Code, Codex, ChatGPT Project, Perplexity Space — whichever you live in. skills.earlybird.com is the human-readable index and the public marketplace.
